What We Learned From Each Brand

Nike
AI-driven demand forecasting. Sole Train rail initiative. Belgium inland waterway (14,000 truck trips replaced). Decentralized regional DCs.
PUMA
Strictest air freight policy (<0.5%). Long-term ocean contracts. Electric trucks on select routes. Digital Zero-100 supply chain initiative.
Skechers
Mumbai DC near JNPT. CJ Logistics 3PL partnership. Contract manufacturing in Asia. US$2,379 FCL benchmark rate.
New Balance
Near-sourcing (Made in USA/UK) for shorter lead times. Seasonal pre-build reduces stockouts. Balanced rail/road usage domestically.
Bata
~95% domestic sourcing. Hub-and-spoke with cross-docking. Milk-run routes. SAP ERP+POS integration. Most India-relevant model.
ASICS
Low DIO via stable demand. Tracks air freight % as a KPI. DC localisation in Southern Europe. Sustainable biodiesel for maritime.
Module 01

Procurement Strategy

8 best-practice pillars derived from Nike, Bata, PUMA, Skechers, ASICS & New Balance ‚Äî each adapted for GenFast's India-based footwear startup context.

01
üèóÔ∏è
Supplier Segmentation &
Centralised Procurement
Best Practice ‚Äî Nike
Adopt Nike-style 3-tier supplier system (Strategic / Preferred / Approved). Use centralised sourcing for negotiation power. Allocate 60% volume to strategic, 30% to preferred, 10% to experimental suppliers.
‚úì Maximises negotiation power and ensures quality & delivery control
02
üåç
Multi-Country Diversified
Sourcing
Best Practice ‚Äî Bata & PUMA
Spread production across India, Vietnam, China & Indonesia to reduce risk. Start with India + Vietnam for cost & reliability. Keep 20% capacity in a secondary country to hedge geopolitical risks.
‚úì Reduces geopolitical, supply, and cost risks while ensuring continuity
03
üå±
Sustainability & ESG
Procurement Standards
Best Practice ‚Äî New Balance
Use recycled EVA/rubber; set ESG audits every quarter; market GenFast as eco-conscious from Day 1. KPIs: % sustainable materials, supplier audit pass rate, waste reduction targets.
‚úì Builds long-term brand equity and meets global sustainability expectations
04
üíª
Digital Supplier &
Inventory Visibility
Best Practice ‚Äî Nike & Bata
Implement Zoho / Oracle NetSuite ERP + supplier portal for real-time lead time, quality, and shipment monitoring. Prevents stockouts and improves responsiveness without heavy IT investment.
‚úì Improves accuracy, reduces stockouts, increases responsiveness
05
üí∞
Cost-Efficient Manufacturing
Partnerships
Best Practice ‚Äî Skechers
Partner with Indian contract manufacturers (Agra, Kanpur, Chennai clusters) for initial batches to maintain margins. Avoid owning manufacturing assets until revenue exceeds ‚Çπ50 Cr.
‚úì Balances cost efficiency with reliable production for scaling
06
‚öôÔ∏è
Quality & Performance
Material Focus
Best Practice ‚Äî ASICS
Use high-grip rubber outsoles, cushioned EVA midsoles; build product performance differentiation vs low-end brands. Quality is GenFast's first moat against established players.
‚úì Ensures durability, comfort & product differentiation
07
üöö
Logistics & Lead-Time
Optimisation
Best Practice ‚Äî PUMA
Use 3PL players like Delhivery / Blue Dart; maintain buffer stock at Mumbai DC hub. Target <5 day replenishment for A-SKUs in metro markets. Structured partner-based logistics networks.
‚úì Reduces lead time, improves delivery reliability & supports scalability
08
üë∑
Labour & Worker
Safety Standards
Best Practice ‚Äî PUMA + Nike + New Balance + Bata
Mandatory Supplier Code of Conduct; third-party social audits annually; worker-voice hotline; fair-wage benchmarks aligned with ILO + local labour laws. Zero tolerance for child labour or forced labour.
‚úì Protects brand from legal/reputation risks; builds investor trust
